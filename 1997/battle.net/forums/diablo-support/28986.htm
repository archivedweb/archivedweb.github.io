> Quite often when I'm playing Diablo on Battle.net, the game all of a

CJ,

Try manually changing your win95 display settings to the ones used by 
Diablo before playing.  DirectX can have trouble switching display 
settings, and manually doing so can fix a number of freezes (as well 
as strange graphic glitches that might occur after Diablo exits).  To 
do this:

right click on your Desktop
select properties
select the settings tab
change your resolution to 640x480
change your colors to 256
click apply -- reboot your computer

If this does not fix things, then delete the file bncache.dat from 
your Diablo folder, and delete all the files in your windows/temp 
folder.  Battle.net accesses these areas, and there could be old or 
damaged data in there that is causing the freeze.

If you're still having problems, then uninstall battle.net (not 
Diablo) using the Add/Remove programs folder in your control panel 
(to get there, click on Start--settings--control panel).  Again, make 
sure the file bncache.dat is deleted from your Diablo folder, and 
make sure your Diablo folder is not read-only.  To do this, Use 
explorer to find your Diablo folder.  Right click on it, then select 
properties.  Make sure the "read only" box is not checked.

If you have further questions, repost here or send email to 
support@blizzard.com.  Give us your system specifications (including 
sound and video cards), describe the problem one more time, and tell 
us what you have done to try to fix things.

Thanks,
Pat N.
