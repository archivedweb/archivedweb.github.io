3) Things I've tried:

a) I always run in 640x480x256 video.

b) I always use the task manager to kill all processes except Systray 
and Explorer.

c) I've delete bncache.dat and removed battle.net.  (I remove 
bncache.dat every time I crash at battle.net)

d) I've changed sound cards.  I was using the onboard Vibra 16 sound 
chip on my ASUS TX97-XE motherboard.  I disabled it, and went out and 
spent $100 on a new AWE 64 Value.

e) I made sure I had the very latest drivers for my sound card, video 
card, and CD-ROM drive.

f) I tried playing without music.  Definitely reduced the CD-ROM 
access, but the crashes still happen.

g) Made sure my SIMMS were matched, timing wise.  I removed my old 
SIMMS and installed 2 identical 16MB SIMMS, 60ns EDO.
    I made sure to set my BIOS for 60ns RAM when I did this.

h) I tried disabling my internal cache, per a suggestion from 
Blizzard tech support (I think it was you, Pat).  I did seem to play

was just an abberation, or a sign of something real.

i) I've tried playing without Music.

j) I tried ripping out IPX, and just playing via TCP/IP over 
battle.net

k) I tried ripping out TCP/IP and just playing via IPX over my local 
network.

l) I've uninstalled Diablo, and reinstalled several times.  Tried 
applying just the 1.03 patch, and playing on my local network.

m)  Tried disabling 3D hardware acceleration on my video card.

n) Tried removing all non-essential stuff from my autoexec.bat and 
config.sy

o) Set buffers=80 in config.sys

p) I tried setting my CD-ROM drive as the slave drive on my primary
