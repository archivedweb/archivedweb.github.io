Aceman,

Some people are not given the multiplayer options when playing Diablo 
(this can happen sometimes after instalilng one of the Diablo 
patches), and I think this problem is what Yi Liu is referring to.  
In this case, as Yi Liu explains, simply reinstalling Diablo (and 
keeping your multiplayer characters) should take care of it.  If this 
does not work, then reinstall one more time, but make sure you are 
not running any extra programs during this process.  Hold down ctl-
alt-del to get to the End Task window.  End everything except 
Explorer and Systray, then go through the resintalling instructions 
found here:
http://www.blizzard.com/support/diablo/reinstall.htm

In your case, however, your game is crashing when you select 
Multiplayer from the Main Menu screen, correct?  This is probably 
linked to your multiplayer character(s) being corrupted.  Take a look 
at my response to your post above concerning this for help in this.
