Actually... no.  First, a coin flip is a bad analogy - since there is
definitely NOT a 50/50 chance of finding a GPOW in each game.

But on that note:  If you plan to flip a coin (infinity) # of times -
that has no effect on the odds.  Your logic is flawed.  If I flip a
coin (infinity-1) times and get all heads - that has no effect on my
next coin flip.  That is, I still have a 50/50 chance of getting heads
on the upcoming flip.

I think where you say 'the more times the coin is flipped...' needs
to be rephrased.  You are confusing the probability of getting
heads on any given flip with the probability of getting heads on
EVERY flip.  Big difference :)

At infinity you MIGHT get a tail - or you might get a head.  Still a
50/50 shot.  That is because we are talking about a single flip.

Your chance of getting ALL heads or ALL tails for multiple flips
decreases as the # of flips approaches infinity.

See the difference?  Maybe you don't - I'm not great at trying
to explain probability.  But trust me - there is a difference.

Sorry to nitpick - I'm just a math geek.
