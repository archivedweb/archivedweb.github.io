Jeff,

You need to run a scandisk and a *full* defragment of your hard disk.

Before you play Diablo, close down all programs that might interfere 
with the game, hit ctrl+alt+del and end task on all programs except 
for Explorer and Systaray.  If you have something running in the 
background, it could be eating up your RAM or conflicting with Diablo.

You need to check and make sure Windows is managing your Virtual 
Memory.  Right Click on your My Computer icon - choose properties - 
choose performance - choose Virtual Memory, make sure the box that 
says let windows manage my Virtual Memory is selected.  If it is not, 
select it and choose OK.

You need at least 100 meg free on the drive you have windows 
installed on, you need at least 50 meg free on the drive you have 
Diablo installed on.  If you have them both installed on one, 150 meg 
is what you need.  If you are running close to that you might be 
getting ready for some problems.

If you still have problems after trying this stuff, post back and we 
will help you further.

Hope this helps,

The Helper
