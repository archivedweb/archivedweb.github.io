> Jason,
> 
> Lazerjedi's probably nailed your modem-to-modem problem.  If you are 
> not using the same version of Diablo, then you will not be able to 
> see your friend's game.  If your friend has connected to battle.net 
> recently, then they are using v1.04 of Diablo.  Because you are still 
> having trouble connecting, you probably are using version 1.0.  The 
> v1.04 patch is available on our web site here:
> http://www.blizzard.com/library/patches.htm
> 
> You should download this patch into your Diablo folder, and run it.  
> This will update your version to v1.04.  If your friend is not using 
> this version, then he should download the patch as well.  Note: the 
> version of Diablo you are currently using is shown in the bottom 
> corner of your main Diablo menu screen.
> 
> Now, to your battle.net connection problem.  I know it is 
> frustrating, but, even though you may have the win95 version of AOL 
> installed on your system, you may be using an older version of AOL 
> when you connect (this can be the case if you've recently installed 
> the win95 version).
> 
> If you've recently downloaded and run setup95.exe from AOL, then you 
> will still have the older version on your computer, and you may be 
> running the old version still (especially if you are using a win95 
> shortcut to connect to AOL).  To make sure you are running the win95 
> version of AOL when you connect, click on Start--Programs--America 
> Online--then "America Online for Windows 95".  Also make sure 
> that you've clicked the "connect now" button (you must 
> actually connect to AOL before you run Diablo).
> 
> Thanks,
> Pat N.
