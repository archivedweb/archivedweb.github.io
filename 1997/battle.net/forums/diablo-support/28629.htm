> In order to play Diablo on Battle.net, you must first connect to the 
> internet.  Connect to the internet, minimize the connection window, 
> start Diablo, choose multiplayer, choose or create your character, 
> choose battle.net and it should connect.  
> 
> You also need to make sure whatever software you use to connect to 
> the internet is 32 bit.  Netscape Navigator 3.0 is an example of such 
> software, it comes with a 32 bit winsock, thats what you need to 
> connect.  I know it sound technical, but that file installs 
> automatically.  If your connection software is already 32 bit, you 
> need to contact whoever is in charge of your firewall.  You need to 
> find out if they are restricting TCP packets on ports 116 and 118.  
> These ports have to be open for in/out TCP packets.  Just tell 
> whoever is responsible for it those ports.  Also, port 6112 has to be 
> open for UDP packets.  In/out also.
> 
> I am sorry if this sounds technical, but its the only way I can 
> really explain it, I don't know anything about firewalls, I just know 
> the ports Diablo uses.
