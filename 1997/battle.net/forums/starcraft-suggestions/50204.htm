> Question
> 
> Can the various spacecraft travel at the speed of light? It seems 
> they would need that capability in order to make interplanetary 
> commerce/war practicable.
> 
> So, if they can travel at the speed of light, wouldn't they be able 
> to outrun any and all light-based weapons systems?

My vote would be for some version of hyperspace for the 
interplanetary travel.  Travel in normal space is rather impractical 
for commerce and war.  Even traveling at close to the speed of light, 
it still takes years for even a trip to a nearby star.  For longer 
distances, by the time you get your state-of-the-art squadron of 
death dealing Starships in position for your coup de gras, your 
opponent has had fifty years of tech upgrades and your now obsolete 
fleet gets pounded to stardust.  Same for commerce.  "Why would 
we want that? It hasn't been in style for years!"
  As to outrunning light-based weapons; can you imagine a dogfight at 
near light speeds?  Oops, overshot my mark by 300,000 km.  Oops, 
where did that asteroid come from?  I was off his course by 0.025 
degrees and can't see him anywhere.

-Amaranthine
