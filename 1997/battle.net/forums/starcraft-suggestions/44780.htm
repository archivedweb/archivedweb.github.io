The initial excitement caused by StarCraft has faded out.  I dont 
know if this something that Blizzard cares about but it is happening. 
 Even us die hard fans of the Blizzard rts games have gotten much 
more cynical.  Blizzards quest for perfection, though admirable, has 
had a side effect of stringing the public on too long.  The magazines 
have lost interest on this game because they have nothing to say. 
There is no updates to publish, the public hungers for new news.  
Take Quake 2 for instance.  I am fairly sure that it has been in the 
works less time than StarCraft, flame away if i am wrong, yet it 
brought out a semi working demo.  The attached readme states that it 
is a "compatibility" test, and there would be bugs.  What 
this means for publicity though is all the Quake fans now have or are 
in the process of downloading the game.  This in turn creates a 
hunger, give people some and they want all.  Blizzard, though the 
uberlists are nice, has not done nearly as much as other companies 
when it comes to grabbing the publics attention.  Even this forum is 
filled with more junk, spam and flames than actual StarCraft talk. 
Why?  We have talked about everything we could based on the 
information given.  Now we just sit and twiddle our thumbs and become 
impatient.
