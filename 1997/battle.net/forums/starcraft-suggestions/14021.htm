No, that would really not be good. Diablo was just a click-click-
click kill all the monsters game and it didn't really matter that you 
knew where you were going.



Good Warcraft 2 maps are carefully designed around good gameplay- 
taking into account player balance, mobility, usually a 
"theme". A random map would SUCK. I mean it would suck hard-
core.  It would just be a scramble of trees, mud, grass, rocks, and 
water, with inaccesible gold mines and wildly unbalanced player 
starting positions. It would have no theme, no style, and no purpose. 
 "Artificial intelligence" still can't design a great map 
like Garden of War, or Rivers, or No Way Out of This Maze.



And I don't know what you mean when you say Internet Warcraft 2 play 
is less fun when you know the map. It's ONLY fun when you know the 
map. I even downloaded maps of the maps and studied them so I 
wouldn't have to play on a map I had never seen. It puts you at a 
major disadvantage, and it's no fun wandering around for 30 seconds 
to find your mine and then building up to a stronghold before you 
realize it's a water map.



I didn't like Diablo's random maps. Maybe that's because I didn't 
like anything about the game, but that's another story. 

What was wrong with it is instead of making each map look different, 
after a while they all looked exactly the same.
