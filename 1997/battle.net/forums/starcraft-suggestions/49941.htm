If you're going to waste time questioning the physics behind a game 
based in a fantastic setting, here's a few things to think about. How 
could any ship in space begin moving at a speed significant enough to 
get anywhere and suddenly perform a fixed-wing atmospheric craft-
quality turn? The object would have to use the same amount of energy 
to slow itself down and then use another huge quantity of energy to 
go in another direction. Zero-gravity fighter craft are an 
impossiblity; any REAL space combat would consist of two huge ships 
firing at each other until one decompressed. Of course, there is 
never going to be any space combat, because attaching weapons to a 
ship capable of leaving the atmosphere would be unbelievably 
expensive and there would be no point to battle over a vacuum when 
the earth is the important thing anyway.

Now that I've pointed out how impossible space combat of any kind is, 
let me make my whole point clear--it's a GAME; if you start sking for 
true physics, then you ask them to completely anull any possibility 
of enjoyment you'd get from it. Just play it and don't ask questions.
