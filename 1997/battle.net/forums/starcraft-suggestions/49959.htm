The field of sci fi has always been influenced by earlier authors and 
inventors.  Submarines (20,000 Leagues Under the Sea), robots 
(Rossum's Universal Robots), Laws of Robotics (Asimov's robot novels 
and novelettes), and armored exoskeleton infantry and mecha (Starship 
Troopers) were the culmination of many people's work.  If anyone 
remebers the Gundam Anime series, which was an intro duction to the 
"big badass bots" that have been the hallmark of Japanese 
Anime, it is dedicated to Heinlen and his book Starship Troopers.  If 
you read the book "Starship Troopers" (published in the 
1950's) it describes troopers bouncing about alien worlds encased in 
robotic armor suits, going on bug hunts and fighting ither aliens.  
The space marine is a natural extension of that genre.  People never 
envisioned that we were going to be fighting in some of the 
environments today.  Although the basic infantry hasn't really 
changed THAT much over the last three centuries, we have certainly 
changed their delivery methods.  We now have armored ground troop 
carriers, airborne paratrooper planes, marine boarding craft and 
other methods of depositing troops here and there.  Every major 
campaign a country for the last three thousand years have really been 
in have been decided by the infantry in the end.  
If we suppose that infantry is needed in the future, the space marine 
really isn't that unfeasible.
