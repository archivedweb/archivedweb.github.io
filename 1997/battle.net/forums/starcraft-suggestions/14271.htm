You are only partially correct. Some units do not directly animate to 
move (like the zeplin or the ships in war2). The speed of these units 
is variable.

It's also possible to speed up and slow down animation so the units 
still remain in sync with their movement. If you've ever seen the 
war2 alpha version, the ogres walked (and animated) slower. 

You are still making a valid point, however, since you can't alter 
animation speed too much. Walking slowly has a different gait to 
running, and just slowing down the animation speed won't work.

Still, it's possible to change speeds quite a bit, especially for 
units like tanks that only need animation speed changed. Units like 
the marines, templars, mechs, etc present more of a problem. 
Might I suggest a second set of animations for these units? 
If they had a set of 'walking frames' they could travel at a slow 
speed. This means that all the units would have enough of a variation 
in speeds so that a group would be able to stay in formation.

It might mean that sometimes a group would move a fraction slower 
than they had to, but it's an acceptable loss.

It probably wouldn't even use much memory, since most of the 
animation frames would be the same. You might only have to add 1 or 2 
frames per unit, and even them only really the walkers.
