On Thu Jul 17 10:25, hermes trismagistus wrote:
> game design / gameplay suggestions
> 
> 1) The rectangular screen sometimes makes it difficult when you are 
> moving up. You can barely see what you are walking into sometimes. 
> Perhaps a round playing area might work better. The controls and 
> buttons would be to the sides since most monitors are rectangular and 
> the circular playing area would take up the whole height of the 
> screen and an equal distance in the middle length of the screen. At 
> any rate, there should be a little more distance between the 
> character and the top edge.
> 
> 2) Probably, already heard it, but the town must go. Or at least get 
> much more compact. Or become a bigger part of playing the game. 
> Personally, I prefer the first of the 3 options. This leads me to an 
> idea for battlenet. When you log on the first screen you see is an 
> overhead view of a large town or city. As you move your mouse over 
> the city it highlights the roofs of various buildings and tells you 
> what they are (no need to have a character moving around in the city 
> though.) There would be a lot of pubs (representing the public chat 
> channels), private clubs for the private chats (the city might have a 
> section outside its walls which could accomadate a changing number of 
> buildings), several shops - a weaponsmith, bowyer, armorer, and 
> apothecary that sell the basic potions and all the normal, nonmagical 
> weapons and armor (but no magical items), and of couse a public 
> trading square (which perhaps would allow for a fair exchange of gold 
> and items.) I think this would give the game a much better atmosphere 
> than the sci-fi like screens bnet presently has and it would take 
> care of some of the basic non-adventure functions without devoting a 
> lot of space or time to it. The city would also have to appear when 
> people start non-bnet games, but the pubs or trading post just 
> wouldn't highlighted. (btw I'm not wild about banks in crpgs and if 
> you don't go with a server based game again it won't matter anyways, 
> but a jewelry shop that sold very expensive gems, rings, or amulets 
> might be another way to allow players to work at amassing and 
> carrying a little more wealth.)
> 
> 3) The four hotkeys worked well for Diablo, but a sequel will 
> probably add more spells (and hopefully skills) and would require 
> more. However, its already kind of akward going from the hotkeys to 
> the belt keys - anything more than 4 keys right next to each other I 
> find aggravating. As an alternative to making 8 or more spell/skill 
> hotkeys how about keys to scroll through your options. Both mouse 
> buttons would have a little window like there is now for the spell to 
> be cast when you right click. The basic move/hack function would be 
> the default on the left button, but since you have to stop to do 
> anything anyways you could still get to some spells/skills from this 
> one as well. This should make a lot of options available quickly 
> without having to fumble around the keyboard (yes, I never got past 
> the training exercise in X- Wing.) Another key could bring up two 
> versions of your spells and skills in two wheels that represent what 
> order they rotate in for each mouse button. You could click and drag 
> them around to your own specifications. Probably, an overcomplicated 
> explanation, but I think this would be much simplier to play than 
> having to use more than 4 keys all around the keyboard (even potions 
> and scrolls could be used this way.)
>
