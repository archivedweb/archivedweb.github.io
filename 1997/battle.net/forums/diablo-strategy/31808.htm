I think I might be able to help you out a bit.  I have been through 
all of those problems with my sorceror (who is a legit level 40 by 
the way).  A sorceror's needs constantly change...early on you just 
need to concentrate on getting your spells advanced and mastering a 
wide variety, all the while hacking away with the weapons of the 
common warrior because you don't have squat for mana.  The middle 
stages are a little easier because not only do you have your favorite 
spells down, but you have some pretty cool melee weapons too.  But of 
course, it's the end stages you are probably worried about.  The 
sorceror is the best character to have in hell...you probably already 
know that.  But the key is WHY he is the best character...magic.  You 
can handle anything you encounter with magic, even those pesky triple 
immune soul-burning, yellow-crap-throwing witches.  Master using 
movement spells, like teleport, and try throwing some well placed 
guardians to weaken them before they get on you.  When they start to 
close in, teleport away and see if you can hit a couple of them with 
stone curse.  Get your whacks in with your sword (get a sword of 
haste if you can), then teleport away again when the heat's on.  It 
may be slow-going, but it's effective.  And remember to be ready to 
throw a town portal if you have to...always have an out.
As far as Chain Lightning goes, there is no relief.  Even with my 
high mana and level 17 CL spell it seems to suck down the juice.  
It's the nature of the spell; you keep this continuous flow of 
lightning going, so it continually drains your mana.  This is an 
important spell though...even monsters that are apparently immune to 
lightning will eventually die if chained enough.
As far as equipment goes, a sword of haste or something that gives 
you fast attack is a must...the rest depends on your preferences.  I 
try to concentrate on raising my magic, mana, and AC.  I figure magic 
can cover me in any situation and I can fall back on my sword to save 
me some mana.
