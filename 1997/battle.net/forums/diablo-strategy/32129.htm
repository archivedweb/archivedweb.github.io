Dizman,
I really don't think there is any way to tell if an identified item 
is an original or a dupe.  But, there are some "common sense" 
rules you
can follow (I'm sure you've probably figured these out, but this may
save some newbies from having to "start over" if they want 
100% legit characters)

  A rule of Diablo that is also a rule of life:  if it looks too good
to be true (legit), it probably is.  Case in point:  someone offers 
to give you an obsidian ring of the zodiac.  It COULD theoretically 
be legit.  In that case, it's extremely unlikely they would have 
another comparable item; if that's true, why would they just GIVE it 
away??

  Related:  as you know, "X of the ages" means it's 
indestructable; some uniques are indestructable, and so is all 
jewelry.  If armor/weapons/staves/etc. do not have the suffix "of 
the ages" and the description says it's indestructable, it's a 
hack, and likely a dupe as well.

  Powerful items just lying arond (king's sword of blood???) are 
probably dupes--who in their right mind would "forget" to 
pick up a KSOB???

  To be save, I myself will ONLY take stuff that's either 
unidentified, or I saw drop on the floor, a trusted companion picked 
up and ID'ed, and offered to me ('cause I needed it more).

  Whimpy items are, I'd guess, probably not duped; why would anyone 
bother?

That's my take.  Some I KNOW are true (the indestructable one, for 
example); the otheres are mainly from reading these posts and just 
thinkin' about it.  If anyone knows a way to tell for sure if a given 
item is a hack/dupe, please, I'd like to know, too!!  (and, if any of 
this is dead wrong, let everyone know!!)
