> Ok, let me address this point. The mage should not throw guardians in

With zig-zagging monsters, the only thing worse than throwing the
guardian behind the monsters is throwing them behind the players!
Bolts from the guardian behind the monsters miss the zagging monster 
and continue on, hitting the players.  Also, bolts fired at monsters 
which are killed (by rogue's arrows or warrior's sword) continue to 
fly, hitting the player(s) who killed the monster, while they are 
trying
to deal with another monster(s) who didn't happen to be the one
targeted by the guardian.

And no, I am _not_ a charge into the fray rogue, I didn't know there 
were such weirdos.  I am a tickle one or two monsters out, kill them 
at range, repeat, rogue.  If more than 1 or 2 monsters get to me it's 
time to bug out.  Retreat to live and fight another day.

> 
> Guardian is also really great against the non-immune (and I mean just 
> non-immune, resist it works too) teleporters. The wizards are kept 
> busy teleporting to fire.

In solo, guardian is a great weapon.  You can place them where
they will do you no harm.  You can use a wall of them to hold off
monsters from getting to you.  The occupy-the-enemy-while-you-
deal-with-them-separately trick you mention above is great.  

They are also useful as scouts - one just inside the doorway ahead.  
You see what direction the bolts go, and probably get a couple 
activated monsters to come out at you where you can deal with them 
in the hallway.  Much safer than in an open room.  (Elemental is even 
better for this)

But in multiplayer play, the guardian-caster is just as responsible 
for every bolt thrown by his guardians as a rogue is for every
arrow from her bow.  Friendly fire from guardians is just as 
inexcusable as from rogues, and complaining that the mage
can't choose where the bolts go is no excuse.  He has the
responsibility of ensuring that the guardian is cast in such a 
position that it will not be able to hit his teammates, or not
cast it at all.

Do you have any suggestions for specific placement of 
guardians in typical combined arms situations?  When you
and a rogue are sitting in the entrance of a "room" in hell,
with a fighter in front of you and monsters closing in from all
sides, where would you put the guardians?  What are the
other players supposed to do while you do your thing?

I have not been able to use it well.  I'm trying to pick your
