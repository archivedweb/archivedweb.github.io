First off, no one knows where you're going to find saintly stuff 
because all the items and their locations are randomly generated.
The biggest difference between single and multi is what happens when 
you die.  In single player, you can just restore the last saved game. 
But in multiplayer, you have to either restart in town or have a 
friend around who will ressurect you.  The thing is-if a monster 
kills you, then you lose half you're inventory.  

In order to play, just click "multiplayer" on the main menu, 
make a new character, and click on Battlenet.  Then bnet will update 
you to the latest version of Diablo.  Then you'll be sent to a chat 
room.  From there you can switch channels, join a game, or create a 
game.  The little bars next to the pictures is lag(or latency).  
green=great  yellow=ok  red=don't bother       The fewer bars, the 
faster the game will go and there won't be much slowdown.Hope I've 
been helpful.  :)
