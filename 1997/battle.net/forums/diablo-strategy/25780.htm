 I have played literally hundreds of games. I have tried all chars 
 types, levels etc. trying to determine if there is any order to the 
 generation of items.

 Basically, I have found really NO difference between items found in 
 Normal, Nightmare or Hell modes. Good stuff usually begins in the 
 caves (I have found 4 windforce bows there and no place else). The 
 best place to get items is definitely level 15 in hell. The reason 
is 
 that there are at least 4 boss's and sometimes 5 on that level. This 
 really ups your chances to find something.

 More than anything else that will effect dropped items is your level 
 and stats. Once you hit around level 38 things start happening 
 quickly. Wirt begins showing you Godly stuff, which by the way, is 
 the ONLY place you will ever have a chance of finding Godly Plate. I 
 have never heard of someone finding from a creature, but I have 
 bought a Godly Helm of Whale from Wirt, so it does exist legally. 
 Wirt also seems to offer Level 38+ warriors more Godly Whale items 
 than the other char types (just as the witch seems to favor a Rogue 
 for cool staffs... go figure).

 Once last thing, pay close attention to this: On my level 44 rogue, 
 the other day a moron used a cauldron and dropped all my stats by 1. 
 Since that game, (I haven't topped off the stats yet), but I have 
 been finding Unique's all over the place! Even normal creatures are 
 dropping unique stuff! In the 3 games since my stats were lowered by 
 1, I have found: Demonspike plate, 2 thinkingcaps, Dreamflange, 
 Eaglehorn, Stormshield, Kings Saber of Haste, Knights Axe of Vampire 
 and a Blessed Crown of Lion. Not too bad for 3 games eh?

 Oh, one more thing, while I play legit most always, I did hack up 
 some chars running experiments on item drops. I was using a Rogue 
the 
 other day with over 1600 mana points due to some tweaked rings, I 
had 
 SEVERAL Dragons items dropped!!!!! 2 Dragon's rings, Dragon's Amulet 
 it was cool! It seemed that the game more often than not, gives you 
items
less than what you already have. Because I had +4000 mana point 
rings, 
the game gave me a mana ring lower than what I already had (which 
happened to be the rare Dragons items).

 So use this info as you will. As a programmer I can say, it all 
comes 
 down to the random seed. The seed is generated from several factors 
 taken from your char. I firmly believe that things are not totally 
 "random". This research was compiled by me in efforts to 
 maximize my own findings. I believe this is very accurate of what 
you 
 can expect in the game.
