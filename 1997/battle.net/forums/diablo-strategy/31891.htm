The only monsters I have encountered that are triple immune are 
advocates and soulburners (yellow witches).  The advocates only exist 
on level 16 and Laz's chamber.  On level 16, there are NEVER 
soulburners.  So using a Golem and your sword on level 16 is EASY for 
a mage (I didn't believe it until my mage was at a high enough level 
to try it).  The reason is that ADVOCATES NEVER TARGET YOUR GOLEM 
WITH FIREBALLS.  THEY ONLY ATTACK WITH FLASH.  The golem is immune to 
flash, so all the mage has to do is stone curse the advocates (from 
behind a wall) and the golem finishes them off easily.

Soulburners SUCK for the mage, because THEY CAN SEE THE GOLEM AND 
SHOOT THEIR YELLOW BLOODSTARS AT HIM FROM A LONG LONG WAYS AWAY.  Its 
absolutely incredible how much they hate your golem!  Additionally, 
the golem alerts the soulburners that someone else is around, so they 
tend to come after you after they kill your golem.  A mage CAN take 
them down, it just uses a LOT of stonecurses, golems, a King's Sword 
of Haste/Speed and an occasional Apocalypse (although this is NOT 
necessary, just helpful).  Soulburners on hell/hell are DEFINITELY 
the one monster where the ROGUE has the advantage over all other 
characters.  She can shoot them  'around the corner' of a hallway.  
Also, while she's hitting them they are stunned and do not shoot 
back.  She has enough magic to stone curse the ones she hasn't gotten 
to killing yet.
  
That's all the clarification needed.  All other monsters are easy for 
the mage in hell/hell because they are susceptible to either chain 
lightning or fireball.  My only question is: WHY AREN'T THERE ANY 
MONTERS IMMUNE TO LIGHTNING AND FIRE, BUT SUSCEPTIBLE TO MAGIC!!??? 
That would make Bloodstar a much more valuable spell in my opinion.
