It is interesting to note how many people believe it is
ok to do something if its just a little but condemn the
exact same activity if it's of larger magnitude.

For example:
    it's ok to give a character a little gold, but not gobs 
        and gobs.
    it's ok to trade between characters of similar level,
        but not between radically different levels
    it's ok to backup chars using the operating system 
        but not to perform the exact same function with
        a custom designed program that just makes it 
        easier.
    or, it's ok to backup, but it may or may not be ok
        to restore based on some judgement of the 
        situation.

So much of this is based on personal judgement,
yet so much criticism is being leveled at other
people whose judgement calls on these issues
differ from ours.

<sigh>

That's why I try not to criticize others, as long as 
they aren't hurting anyone else.  I just take notes
as to who makes the calls similar to me in case 
I ever bump into them and have a chance to play.

That's also why I don't consider anything you can
do with the (unhacked) program to be cheating,
but anything that requires external utilities is.  My
judgement of whether or not to do any of these
things is based on what it will do to my enjoyment
of the game, and whether or not anyone else is
hurt by it.  It's that simple.
