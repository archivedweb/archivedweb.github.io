As with lightning, each reading of a Chain Lightning book increases 
the length of the bolt.  That being the case, each time something is 
hit, it gets hit often (usually)  Also, a less known fact about Chain 
Lightning, is that the higher the level, the farther away a monster 
can be, and still be targeted by the chain lightning.  Eventually 
(like, oh, after you reach level 45 or so) you'll learn some tactics 
to use when casting Chain so that you won't have the huge gaps in it.

Little known use for golems.  Enemy finders.  Send one out into an 
open room, and all the witches will start firing at him from 
everywhere, but he won't be close enough to trigger them to move.  A 
nice (and little known) way to figure out roughly what you're up 
against (so you can decide to run or not)  hehehehe.

Another seldom known fact is that Nightmare Hell still gives 
experience to level 46 mages.  So, why bother with hell/hell when you 
can just go to nightmare/hell and get more experience per hour there.
