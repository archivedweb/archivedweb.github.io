Based on observations made both as a warrior and a mage, the best 
thing a warror can do is "always move slowly and deliberly". 
Don't go charging from one side of a battle to another seeking things 
to hit... you'll invariably run into someone's field of fire. A 
warrior should follow one of two tactics:

1. If facing melee creatures move slightly ahead and to one side of 
the ROP (Rest Of Party). By doing this the melee creatures should 
target you... not the rogue/mage. Because you are off slightly to one 
side they should both have missle opprotunities while the creatures 
close. Once they close -- DON'T MOVE! Stand you're ground and hack 
away. This will allow your rogue/mage to target other creatures (such 
as the witches) without hitting you. A good rogue can line-up and 
pick-off some of the creatures swarming you without worrying about 
hitting you -- PROVIDED YOU DON'T MOVE. However, for this to work, 
two things need to be established. You need an AC/Blocking/Haromony 
sufficient to be swarmed without falling immediately (more on this 
below). Also, you need to let your parterns know how many "at 
once" you can handle. They should watch what swarms you and if 
your "at once" level is exceeded should start stone cursing 
to help you. They should not stone curse unless your "at 
once" level is exceeded... its a waste of mana and condecending 
(look guys, I can handle one lone blood knight... OK?).

2. If facing ranged creatures like witches and advocates, either 
stand back and let the rogues/mages take them. This is often best 
because when the warrior advances to engage these creatures he 
usually just activates more trouble. If you must engage, then pick a 
wall and move along it taking out anything in your path. By doing 
this you move predicably... so the rogues/mages can adjust for your 
actions and not fry you. Also, you leave the center of the room free 
to fill up with wizzing arrows and fireballs -- it's really not a 
place you want to be.

Notes on AC: By my experiences, a warrior with a Harmony item, a 
regular-speed shield, and an AC >= 170 can stand up to just about 
any number of melee types (knights/vipers/etc.) in normal/hell. He'll 
take damage and use potions from his belt -- but he won't immediately 
fold. I had to get my AC up around 250 to gain similar staying power 
in nightmare/hell.  I estimate I'll need fast block and an AC up over 
300 for hell/hell -- though I'm just guessing. With my champions 
sword of haste (bastard) I can take any creature on one-on-one or 
even two-on-one in hell/hell -- but I can't yet stand up to being 
swarmed. As I said above, if you are below these protection 
"thresholds", you have to let your parterns know when to step 
in and help you. Likewise, they need to know you aren't quite the 
"speed bump" they remember all those dupers to be.
