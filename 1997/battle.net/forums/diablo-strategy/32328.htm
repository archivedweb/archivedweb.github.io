killed him. I reloaded the game, killed him again, reloaded, killed

holds for Multi as you can't (legally anyway) save out the game and 
"re-kill" a creature. 

I believe that at least in multi-player, the level is seeded via 
creatures by the person who enters the level first. I'm gonna take a 
HUGE guess here and guess that each unique creature works 1 of two 
ways. #1), for each player in the game, it has a possible item to 
drop if killed by a player (much like Wirt, Griz and Adria all have 
seperate lists of items for each char in the game). *OR* it just

debugger to see what it does.... if I get time time).

It's all fun to think about though... in the end, it all comes down 
to "randomness" as to what item will be dropped. External 
things like, char attribs, class, char state, items carried, items 
worn can all "effect" the random value (that's how I 
implemented some intensive Ball Catch logic in the Football game I'm 
wrapping up. You get a random number but the ranges of what that 
value mean are determined via states in the game (ie: in situation X, 
there is a 50% chance a ball will be caught or deflected, if not 
deflected, there is a 50/50 chance that the ball will either be 
caught or intercepted so that translates into a 50/25/25 table. If we 
assume the offensiver player has a greater catch "attribute", 
we slide the table slightly in his favor to say: 50/30/20. 

I do not know what method Bliz uses but it is very obvious that items 
worn by the char and char class and exp determine alot of randomness.
