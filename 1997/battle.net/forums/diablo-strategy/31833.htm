I'm tired of all the arguments I get into with you. Most of my posts 
seem to gather alot of interest expecially my last survey. I got alot 
of mail from people who enjoyed that (44 replies last time I counted, 
darn near a record). I think alot of people enjoy our little 
"debate" but I think we are going too far.


Anyway I will state my position 1 last time for everyone: I am an old 
timer. I detested cheaters. Cheating started the day diablo came out, 
long before anyone ever "created" a cheat. Anyone remember 
"Modem drop dup"? How about "Portal Dup"? These 
existed from the beginning and people took advantage of them. I hated 
them for it. I was a purist.

As the cheating escalated in later months, the programmer side of me 
(I'm a lead game programmer if anyone doesn't already know) began to 
really get interested in how alot of these simple cheats worked. I 
began to explore their methods and actually talked with their 
creators. I talked to Enigma, Andy Duplain and others. It was 
facinating to me. 

At my work, with Diablo cheating making all the news, I was asked to 
explore how these cheats work and create a model of a game that was 
cheat proof that could be used in future projects.

Somewhere around this time, I began backing up my char due to a 
flakey system. I knew it was cheating but I rationalized that as long 
as I don't restore my char after loosing items to creatures, it's 
really not "cheating" .... yea right *wink wink nudge 
nudge*<g>.

In the world of Tristram, me in my Awsome Plate, I beat Nightmare 
mode solo. I then beat Hell mode solo. Soon after, I became very good 
making solo Hell Hell runs. Although always a challenge, my 
experience was enough I knew when something was impossible and to 
retreat, or when to continue. I learned alot of tricks to stay alive. 
Armor actually became irrelevant in my method of gameplay as I was 
more akin to a mage than warrior. Spells and a Bow are everything.

I began to want something fresh so I began playing the single player 
game. On my first try, I was able to start in the church with a new 
char and work my way slowly, all the way down to Diablo and kill him. 
The quests were really cool so I continued to play, wanting to see 
each and every char. I wanted to learn EVERYTHING about Diablo a fire 
was kindled in me to know all. 

At a rare moment, I found an OZ, at another, GPOW from wirt. I always 
knew QPOW was the armor of choice for dupers and wanted to try it. I 
imported it back over to my Multiplayer char and started 
experimenting. I got a GCOW from wirt and shortly there after a GHOW 
from wirt again. I tried an experiment to see what a 500hp rogue 
would be like. I was very disappointed. But, call me lazy, I still 
kept the GPOW on my multiplayer char and continued playing, having 
fun.

By this time, cheating was in a frenzy and I became very flexible to 
cheats. I never have believed in the hardcore cheats like AutoKill, 
TownKill, GodMode, 256 level Spells, LeachKill and such. I started 
meeting alot of people who had dups to no end and as we played... it 
was still fun, NO I take that back, it was a HELL of a lot of fun. 
They always died several times to my 1 because I had the better 
experience, but it was still enjoyable.

I figured that hey, as long as they aren't trying to PK me, they 
aren't distracting from my fun... in fact, alot of times they added 
to it.

I started digging into the deep roots of Diablo. I have seen the 
hidden movies. I have seen the hidden Art. I have heard the hidden 
WAV files. I have see the hidden creatures. Best of all, I have 
actually PLAYED against the hidden creatures.

Yes, I am a cheater. I don't care who knows it. My love for Diablo is 
very great... being able to use the existing cheats to see these 
marvelous "hidden" things is great. The fact that Raymond 
figured out how to enable the hidden creatures for game play still 
amazes me. I have seen more and done more in Diablo that most people 
ever will. (damn, anyone used that cool Special Effects cheat? Simply 
incredible)
I do not agree that all cheats are "evil" though.

If I were to give advice to anyone starting out in Diablo, I 
definitely would recommend them to play legit. If they do however, 
want to learn more about Diablo or use a *gasp* cheat... well its 
their right. 

DarkSniper, we will never agree on most issues. I find it ironic that 
in 3 areas of my survey that I concider cheating, you do not. But, 
this is a "free" game. I respect your wishes to be legit and 
wish you well. I only ever wanted the same in return.

In the end, I'm going to "bury the hachet" on this. I'm not 
leaving but I do not want to drag out this debate much further. We 
both opened insights on cheating that the others readily enjoyed.

Good luck in Diablo, Good luck with your clan. Cheers.
