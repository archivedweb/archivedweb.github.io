I agree with you on 2 and 3, but while I agree that giving elixers to
low level players may be a bad idea, it is not cheating.  How is it
different from 2 or 3 except in the nature of the item being given?
And borrowing your portal argument below, if Blizzard did not want
low level characters to benefit from elixirs, it simply could have 
made them ineffective (this appears to be an artifact of the 
development of the game as a single player game, where a player
would not be able to get elixirs from any other source than
Adria, who would not sell them until level 26).  

Please explain the difference between 2, 3, and 4, and how 4 fits
within your definition of cheating but 2 and 3 do not.

I agree with you on 5, but not on 6.  To answer your hypothetical, 
there is no difference between restarting games to get easier 
creatures and restarting to get different items.  Neither is cheating 
(as I reasoned in my reply to your survey, starting a new game is a
part of the game design, and your intent in restarting is irrelevant).
Again, please explain how this fits within your definition of 
cheating. 
In particular, please answer the hypothetical questions I asked in
my reply, such as when can you restart without it being cheating? 
Do you have to clear all 16 levels?  Is is cheating to start a game
to do a Laz run (i.e., ignoring all but levels 13-15)?  Is is cheating
for a low level character to complete the lower levels of the 
dungeons (i.e., church and cats) without completing or attempting
to complete the more difficult levels?
