On Thu Oct 23 13:29, bikejr wrote:
> Does increased armor class simply reduce the chance that you will get 
> hit, or does it also reduce the damage when you get hit? Also does it 
> help at all against magic/fire/lightning or are you better off going 
> in with max resistances at the expense of high AC? 


The generally accepted formula in this forum (with a detractor or 
two) is that 

H = 1 / (MHt - AC), for MHt > AC

where H = percentage of time you are hit
  MHt = attacking monster's base "To Hit" probability
  AC  = your armor class

Other factors do affect H though.

It seems to me that once you get your AC up to MHt, you won't get hit 
if you're standing still and being attacked by one opponent.  If 
there are more than one and you're using a shield, your blocking 
action on one mob will allow the other mob a greater chance to hit 
you.

Something that tests on this forum have shown is that AC *does* seem 
to reduce fire damage from range attacks.  Unfort I don't remember
