On Tue Jul 15 12:49, Zamp (Snakegod) wrote:
> Before responding, I want to make clear that I define 
> "cheating" as the use or exploitation of external 
> programs/hacks or internal bugs/anomalies in a way that is contrary 
> to the intentions, structure, limitations, or restrictions of the 
> game as created and designed by the game's designers or creators.
> 
> I also do not consider "cheating" to be automatically bad or 
> good, or right or wrong.  But then, there is gaming behavior that 
> some people consider "bad" or "wrong" that is not 
> cheating.
> 
> With that in mind . . . 
> > 
> > #). Is it Cheating to accept Gold from another player?
> 
> No, as long as the gold is not duped.  Trading gold (and other items) 
> is a game function.
> 
> > #). Is it Cheating to accept Items from a higher char?
> 
> No, as long as the items are not duped.  Trading items (like gold) is 
> a game function.  Blizzard placed no restriction on intercharacter 
> trading.  The only restriction placed on low level characters' use of 
> items is the stat requirement for that item (there is no level 
> requirement, as there is for game difficulty).
> 
> > #). Is it Cheating to have higher level chars buy items for you?
> 
> No.  This is actually just trading/accepting items (the previous 
> question) with the source of the item specified.  It makes no 
> difference whether the higher level char. found, bought, or traded 
> for the item, as long as it's legit.
> 
> > #). Is it Cheating to have higher level chars give lower level chars 
> > elixers?
> 
> No.  This is actually just trading/accepting items (question 2) with 
> a particular item specified.  It makes no difference what the item 
> is, as long as it's not duped.
> 
> > #). Is it Cheating to clear levels for lower players?
> 
> No.  Cheating really doesn't depend on the relative levels of the 
> players involved.  Is it cheating for a lower level player to clear a 
> level while a higher level clears another (or sits in town)? Or what 
> about two characters of the same level?  Of course not.  
> Incidentally, it's hard to see what benefit the lower level player 
> gains from this, since they don't gain XP.  Going into the level and 
> picking up gold and items?  But this is effectively the same as if 
> the higher level player picked up the items and gold and gave them to 
> the lower level player in town, which isn't cheating.  This just 
> removes the higher level player as the middle-man.
> 
> > #). Is it Cheating to Start games with only the intent to check Wirt, 
> Adria or Griz for items?
> 
> No, because starting new games is part of the game design.  Your 
> intent in starting the game doesn't matter.  (If you think that this 
> is cheating, where do you draw the line?  How much of the game must 
> you play before it wouldn't be considered cheating?  All of it?  What 
> about characters who can handle the Church or Cats, but not the Caves 
> or Hell?  Are they cheating by clearing only the Church or Cats?  And 
> what about people who do "Laz runs"?  Are they cheating by 
> starting games only with the intent of doing a Laz run?)
> 
> > #). Is it Cheating to use Deye in chat to look at other peoples stats?
> 
> Technically yes, because an external program is being used inside the 
> game to gain information that you otherwise would not be able to gain 
> inside the game using normal game functions.  That's not to say its 
> use is good or bad, however, since its use may be justified in some 
> cases.  But it is "cheating."
> 
> > #). Is it Cheating to allow lower players to portal into areas they 
> > could not regularly access?
> 
> No, because town portals are part of the game design, and the game 
> design does not prevent lower level characters from entering portals 
> to these areas (compare with Blizzard preventing lower level 
> characters from entering higher difficulty games).  Unless this has 
> been identified as a "bug" like duping, it is not cheating.  
> (As far as I am concerned, if a lower level character wants to risk 
> their items by taking on a very difficult level, good luck!).
> 
> > #). Is it Cheating to create chars whos only purpose is to hold extra 
> items?
> 
> No.  Creating characters is a legitimate game function, and as 
> discussed above, giving items from one character to another is part 
> of the game design.  Intent again is irrelevant in this case.
> 
> > #). Is it Cheating to transfer items found by 1 char to another char?
> 
> No.  If it's not cheating to transfer items under question 2 above, 
> it isn't cheating to transfer items between characters created by the 
> same person.
> 
> > #). Is it Cheating to Pk with the Nova spell?
> 
> No.  No more so than PKing by any other method can be considered 
> cheating.  Nova is a legitimate spell in the game.  (Note that 
> duelists can agree to a "Code Duello" that establishs any 
> restrictions they desire, and a violation of those rules could be 
> considered cheating, although not as I defined it above.)
> 
> > #). Is it Cheating to Portal Kill someone?
> 
> No.  See previous answer.
> 
> > #). Is it Cheating to Teleport in town?
> 
> Yes, because to do so requires the use of an external program that 
> allows you to do something in the game that is not part of the game 
> design.  Whether the time you save by teleporting in town makes this 
> "cheat" a "bad" or "harmless" cheat is 
> another question.
> 
> > #). Is it Cheating to backup chars against system crashes?
> 
> No.  This is one of the cases where the intent of the user makes a 
> difference because it involves the use of an external program. While 
> the use of an external program allows you to save char data, using it 
> to combat against external problems or events (like a system crash) 
> does not accomplish something contrary to the intent or design of the 
> game.  I believe that this is apparent from Blizzard's help to people 
> in saving and transferring character data.  As long as the intent and 
> result are the same, whether you follow Blizzard's instructions 
> (which are NOT part of the game, by the way) or use a hack program 
> should make no difference.
> 
> 
> Zamp
> Snakegod
