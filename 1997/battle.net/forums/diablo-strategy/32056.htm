On Wed Jul 16 20:13, Anonymous wrote:
> Here are my opinions on cheating, I appreciate feedback on whether or 
> not you people agree, or disagree with my thoughts, so here goes:
> 

I usually don't like to get involved in these discussions, but here 
are my opinions...

> 
> 1.)  Using any program that alters your character IS cheating (ex:  
> raising exp, gold, stats, spell levels, or adding items)
> 

Agree.

> 2.)  Backing up/Restoring multiplayer characters is NOT cheating, and 
> I am not going to use the old "system crash" excuse.  In 
> single player Diablo, if you get your ass whipped by some demons, all 
> you have to do is restore from your last save, well, there is NO 
> reason that multiplayer should be any different, especially with the 
> added risk of pkillers, and the like.
>

Sure it is. In multi-player, your death has an effect on others. If 
you die
and lose your precious stuff to another player, why should you be able
to use a backup to recover your KSOH/Dreamflange/Windforce or
whatever great items you've lost? That item belongs to that other 
player
now.

This would be another way to 'dupe' items - I'll just enter a game, 
drop
that Obs Ring of Zod,  then leave the game and recover my character.

As for Pking, it's part of the game. Blizzard put that 'player attack'
button there for a reason. You just have to watch your back. In life,
we get backstabbed all the time, why should it be any different in a
game that requires interaction with other players.

> 3.)  Transferring characters to/from single player and multiplayer is 
> NOT cheating.  If the character has not been altered by external 
> programs, or hacked in some way, then this transfer is perfectly 
> legitimate, after all, why should someone spend weeks, building up a 
> single player character, just to find out that if they want to play 
> multiplayer, then they will have to start all over from level 1!
> 

There are items in the single-player games that are not available in
the multi-player games. So I think it's cheating.

> 4.)  In multiplayer games, high-level players, giving things to lower 
> level characters is alright, IF they are normally obtainable by that 
> lower level player, such as a level 20 rouge, giving a level 5 
> warrior a few healing potions, but it should not be takken too far!  
> Such as giving a plate of armor that the lower level character would 
> not have access to for levels to come.  I am sick and tired of seeing 
> extremely low level characters such as levels 1-12 having Godly 
> plates of the Whale, and Obsidian rings of the Zodiac, and Dragon's 
> amulets of the zodiac, and King's swords of haste, these things are 
> EXTREMELY rare and should not be found untill really high levels, I 
> get on B-net all the time, and I always see this, level 1-12 people 
> having better equipment than me (level 16 Warrior), and you know 
> someone just gave it to them.
> 

If the item is 'legit', I see no problem with giving it away. It will 
make
the game very boring for the low level characters.

> 5.)  Dupping IS cheating.  If people did not dupe, then the problem
