Especially since it looks like I have to eat a little humble
pie here. After quite a bit of thought, I have to agree with
Zamp's statement that there is:

"no difference between copying the specific data files by 
 DOS or by Kevin's program and copying these files as part
of a larger group (up to and including the entire hard disk)"

Now, I have to say that I still _feel_ there is a difference between
backing up my Diablo files while doing my weekly tape back up
(Ok -- I admit it -- my annual tape back up), and backing them up
using DiabloBackup.exe --- but it's not sustainable by any
rational argument I can see.

That being so, as Zamp and a number of others pointed out,
it is inconsistent to maintain that using DiabloBackup.exe to
backup/restore corrupted Diablo files is "cheating" but using
a full system backup to do the same thing, among others,
 is not.  And while I appreciated Lok's agreement on "backup as
cheating" and admire his consistency in not restoring
characters after a hard drive crash, in no way shape or form
could that be called "cheating".

What does this do for the whole question of what is and isn't
"cheating" --- well once you say some external programs are
not "cheats" I don't see any way to  draw a line, other than
how you want to play the game, and it becomes pretty meaningless
(cheers from the side!).  Is Deye cheating? It's a third party 
program,
does stuff  Bliz's Diablo program doesn't do, is that 
"cheating"? hmm...I dunno. It's not "cheating" to 
restore my character
after a crash --- what if I just lost my MBOH to lag (I wish!)? Bliz 
didn't
intend me to loose stuff to lag, and it hardly affects the game
play at all,  so it wouldn't be "cheating" to restore.
I just got MK'ed -- Bliz didn't mean that to happen either...

Finally, I agree with Lok on another thing: the main reason not to 
backup
is nothing to do whether it's "legit" or not --- the game's 
just a heck of a lot
more exciting when you have no safety net (even if you never fall 
off).
