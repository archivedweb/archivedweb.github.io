On Mon Jul 14 20:57, Lorax wrote:
> I have long been reading this forum, and the sheer volume of posts on 
> the subject of "cheating vs. legit" in Diablo never ceases to 
> amaze and annoy me.  It seems that the land of Tristram has been 
> divided into two distinct sects, cheaters and legit players.  I, 
> however, believe that the distinction is not as clear as many would 
> like to think.  This post details my thoughts on the subject.  Call 
> it an opinion, call it a vent, call it what you will.  
> 
> First, let us examine the typical "cheater."  What drives a 
> person to cheating?  Maybe frustration with their inability to play 
> well.  Maybe a need to dominate others to boost their ego.  Or 
> perhaps just boredom with the game.  Whatever the reason, they have 
> chosen to alter the game for themselves and/or others.  Their methods 
> are many.  Duping, hacking items, godmode, ghostmode, inflating their 
> attributes or spells; just to name a few.  Some are good-natured.  
> Some are malevolent and rampant pk's.  They are the bane of the 
> "legit" player.
> 
> The typical "legit" player is the "anti-cheater."  
> They detest duping, hacks and other "impure" methods of play. 
>  To them, cheaters have ruined the game as it was intended.  They 
> often consider themselves above the cheater, and are generous with 
> flames that state the fact.  Some have even dedicated themselves to 
> "rescuing" newbies from the pitfall of cheating.  Many join 
> guilds where they can play legit games with their legit friends.  
> They are often thought of as the "good guys" of battlenet.
> 
> Now, at first inspection, these two groups may seem all-inclusive.  
> But I propose that there is a third group lost somewhere in between.  
> They are less apparent, but are possibly the greatest in number.  We 
> have sometimes seen glimpses of these players in posts here titled, 
> "Is this cheating?", etc.  They are players who selectively 
> do things that other players may consider cheating.  I place myself 
> in this middle group, and I would argue that most people who claim to 
> be "legit" belong here, too.
> 
> As an example, I will use a recent post from this forum.  
> [Disclaimer:  The use of this individual as an example does not imply 
> that I am picking on him, and does not require a flame in return.  :-
> )]  The poster claims outright to be "legit."  He explains 
> that he recently convinced a newbie to cast off cheating and join him 
> in legitimacy.  As part of this, he instructed the newbie not to 
> accept duped or hacked items.  The two played together, but the 
> newbie played the church while the poster went to hell.  The poster 
> returns with items (rings and armor) from hell and gives them to the 
> newbie to help him stay alive in the catacombs.  Now, I would claim 
> that the newbie's acceptance of these items jeopardizes his 
> reputation as a "legit" player.  Would he have gotten these 
> items on his own at his level?  Mind you, I'm not saying this is 
> WRONG.  In fact, I admire the poster's intentions.  I'm just saying 
> that it isn't (in my opinion) 100% "pure."
> 
> But on to the point.  I see legitimacy, not as a divergent issue, but 
> as a CONTINUUM.  Put simply, it is not a black and white issue.  It 
> is an expansive range of shades of gray.  At one end is the 
> "extreme cheater" who uses every hack in the book.  At the 
> other end is the "true legit."  In my opinion this is one who 
> does not use any cheats/hacks/accessory programs, does not dupe, and 
> does not use anything that he does not kill or buy himself.  Now I'm 
> sure that many of you are thinking, "That's ridiculous.  Your 
> standards for a 'true legit' are too high."  In response, I would 
> say that it's supposed to be.  It's an extreme, the end of the 
> continuum.  There is no implication, on my part, that you should even 
> TRY to be a "true legit."  
> 
> I propose the following examples to illustrate this idea. They are 
> roughly ranked in order from most legit to least legit.  [Disclaimer 
> #2:  This list is not intended to be all inclusive, nor is the order 
> of ranking necessarily "gospel."]
> 
> *  "True legit."
> *  Backing up characters in case of system crashes.
> *  Taking legit items from other players.
> *  Taking legit items from higher level players.
> *  Using hacks for protection against cheating pk's.
> *  Creating, using, giving, and taking duped items.
> *  Creating, using, giving, taking, and duping hacked items.
> *  Hacking stats, spells, etc.
> *  Godmode, etc.
> *  "Extreme cheater."
>   
> And now, my second point (this is the venting part).  The legitimacy 
> continuum is in no way related to being good or bad.  Being a cheater 
> doesn't necessarily make you BAD.  And being "legit" 
> certainly doesn't make you better than everyone else.  I don't know 
> which is worse, pk'ing cheaters who ruin games, or so-called 
> "legits" on their high horse throwing around flames.  For 
> example, how many times have I heard the "press Alt-F4 to 
> dupe" line, or, even worse, attempts to try to get cheaters to 
> reformat their hard drives?  If, as a "legit", you really 
> want to be a "good guy", throwing around childish insults and 
> being downright destructive is not the way.  Don't play with people 
> if you don't approve of their place in the continuum.  But let them 
> play the way they want.  Everyone (well, almost) has payed for the 
> game, just like everyone else.  And everyone has the right to enjoy 
> the game however they wish. 
> 
> As an attempt at setting a good example, I'll relate a recent 
> experience of mine.  I started a public game on battlenet.  I almost 
> always begin a night on battlenet in a public game, usually titled 
> "TheTruffulaGrove.pureonlyplease" (feel free to drop in 
> sometime if you want.)  Anyway, I was playing with my warrior, and I 
> was soon joined by a rogue who was near the same level as I.  I went 
> to town to meet her, and she claimed to be "legit".  I 
> noticed, however, that her hit points seemed a little high.  "Oh, 
> well," I thought, "we'll give it a try."  We went to the 
> catacombs, and played fairly well together.  She was considerate, and 
> cooperative.  I was enjoying myself.  But quite suddenly, I wasn't 
> having much fun.  I looked at my health, and I was almost dead.  She 
> had accidentally shot me with ONE arrow, and it almost killed me.  I 
> popped open a portal, and jumped in.  She said, "what are you 
> doing?"  I told her, "your damage is just too high, you 
> almost killed me with one shot."  She assured me that she was 
> "legit" ... she had just gotten some good items from a 
> friend.  I explained that I was sorry, but I just wasn't comfortable 
> playing with her.  I thanked her for playing with me, bid her good 
> venturing, and left to start a new game, 
> "TheTruffulaGrove2.pureonlypleas".  Now maybe she was using 
> hacked equipment.  Maybe not.  It didn't matter.  There was no need 
> to accuse, there was no need to preach.  The very next game, I met 
> "Rook", a mage with a gaming attitude similar to mine.  We 
> played well together, but were soon pk'ed by an obvious cheater :-(.  
> We didn't have to flame him.  We simply quietly moved on to a private 
> game, and had a great time playing together for the rest of the 
> evening.
> 
> Now, to sum up this long story, it was a normal night on battlenet.  
> I met a polite "cheater", I met an impolite 
> "cheater", and I met a polite "legit".  I didn't 
> flame, I didn't judge.  I simply played with the person I was 
> comfortable with, and had a good time.
> 
> When I first decided to write this post, the forum was overrun with 
> people preaching about the evils of cheating, flaming any cheater who 
> asked for help.  Today, though, I noticed several posts from people 
> who seem to have the "live and let live" attitude that I try 
> to keep.  (Lestat, Code Myster, and Saint Michael, just to name a 
> few.)  They make this post a little like old news, but I'm glad to 
> see them.  I truly believe that if more people followed this way of 
> thinking, battlenet would be a better place.
> 
> You might want to know where I believe I fit into the continuum.  On 
> a scale from 0-10 (why does everyone always leave out 0?), with 0 
> being the "extreme cheater", and 10 being the "true 
> legit", I would rank myself at 8 or 9.  In the past, I used the 
> trainer to see ghosts, but recently ditched it after I noticed my 
> warrior's spell levels jumped to 16 for no apparent reason.  (I had 
> to set all spells to level 0 and start my book hunt over to feel 
> comfortable with myself).  I back up my characters regularly and 
> restore them when I lose items to server problems or cheating (but 
> not legit) mk's.  I have accepted items from a friend who I play with 
> often who is near the same level as I.  Otherwise, I'd say I'm pure.  
> Am I a cheater?  Am I legit? ... Does it matter?  I would argue that 
> the answer to all three questions  is "no."
> 
> Finally, I'd like to thank you for you patience , if you're still 
> reading :-).  I realize this post is VERY long (I might have a record 
> here), but this stuff has been welling up inside me for a long time, 
> and finally getting it out feels good.  I realize that this post may 
> offend some people.  Please know that this is not my intention.  I 
> also realize that this post will soon be lost to that great bit-
> bucket in the sky.  Cheating pk's will continue to ruin the fun for 
> others.  "Legits" on their high horses will flame away.  I 
> only hope that I have made you think for a moment about the value of 
> tolerance.
> 
> So, in conclusion, I think this "legit vs. cheater" argument 
> is way out of hand.  Legitimacy is a continuum, not a finite state.  
> In fact, many players who claim to be "legit" are not 
> completely pure.  My advice to you ... Find a place in the continuum 
> where you are comfortable playing, play with people who you are 
> comfortable with,  and do not judge others for their choice.  
> 
> 
> Lorax [lvl 39 mage, semi-retired]
