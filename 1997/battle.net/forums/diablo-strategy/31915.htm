>   This is something I've been thinking about for a long time now, and 
> I've gotta get it out. Lots of people seem to think only in terms of 
> Good/Bad, Legit/Cheat, Co-op/PK. Each duality is considered the same 
> (ie Good=Legit=Co-op; Bad=Cheat=PK), but I think we really need to 
> think along two axis instead of one. I once played a great deal of 
> AD&D, and Diablo has a lot of similarities to the old tabletop 
> classic. So, what I'm considering here is a WHOLE NEW idea: using the 
> AD&D alignment system to kill two birds with one stone: (1) quickly 
> divide people up into those you do(n't) want to play with, and (2) 
> make cheating part of the roleplaying experience available to Diablo 
> players. First off, Legit/Cheat isn't about good and evil, it's about 
> following the rules. Thus, Lawful and Chaotic are the new terms of 
> the day. Co-op/PK can be described as good and evil, though. Either 
> you work with people, or you prey upon them. Even this isn't iron-
> clad, however; remember duelling, self-defense, and avenging honor 
> are "good" reasons for player killing.
> 
> Some examples, which may be construed as "picking on" if you 
> wish, although such is not my intent.
> 
>    Lawful Good: DarkSniper, obviously. Also, however remember that 
> less verbose posters also qualify as LG, just because you don't 
> preach a sermon, doesn't mean you can't keep the faith.
>    Lawful Neutral: I've yet to see this creature. Stays away from 
> cheats, but has no compunctions against wasting a fellow player, 
> especially if they are annoying in some manner. May attack from 
> suprise, but probably won't.
>    Lawful Evil: The image projected by the Priests of Chaos guild. 
> They don't cheat, but they'll use every trick in the book to kill 
> other players. I can respect that. (Not that I'd knowingly play in a 
> game with one.)
>    Neutral Good: These are the newbie-helpers. They dupe things, or 
> hand out unneeded items to new players. Or, perhaps, like myself, 
> maintain back-ups in case of crashing.
>    True Neutral: Haven't seen this person yet either. Probably just 
> not interested in anything beyond single player, though.
>    Neutral Evil: These are the people who like killing other players, 
> and are just to that stage where it would be a little easier if they 
> could dupe off that ring they found, or use Deye to pick easy 
> targets. They stack the deck, but don't use marked cards . . . . yet.
>    Chaotic Good: Code Myster. He uses the cheats, he admits to it,
