On Thu Oct 23 09:59, Aurium wrote:
> Just what is seeding and how is it done?? Sorry if this is a stupid 
> question, I am just curious.

It's also referred to as overseeding or top seeding.  It's usually 
done in the Fall after a lawn has been aerated.  Try to collect all 
the soil plugs prior to speading the seed.  In cooler climates I 
recommend 31" Tall Fescue.  Make sure your seed is at least 
98% pure.  More than 2% weed seed can cause you problems.  
Using a caster spreader, a setting of 5-7 should be right, depending 
on your model.  Most seed companies have a chart on the bag for 
spreader settings.  One pound should cover approx. 1000 sq. ft.  
Water heavily after seeding and keep the ground moist, but not wet, 
at least 3-4 weeks.  Ground cover, such as straw, should not be 
necessary.  Growth should start appearing in a week to 10 days.  When 
top seeding, don't expect to get more than 20-25% germination.  
Do not fertilize young grass as it can kill it.  In the Spring, water 
your new growth heavily and fertilize as usual.  Remember, a thick 
lawn is on weed comtrol.

Hope this was helpful.  If you have any further questions please
