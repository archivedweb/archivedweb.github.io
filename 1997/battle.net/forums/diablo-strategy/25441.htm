     I really can't believe some of the butchered English that gets 
posted on this forum.  Note that I am not talking about people who 
don't speak English as their native language - hats off to them for 
posting at all in a foreign language.  I'm complaining about the 
people who should, theoretically at least, know better.  I'm also not 
talking about typos etc, since we all do that.

     The other day I posted something suggesting that "your the 
moron" was rather poor English, when someone replied insisting 
that "your" was correct.  Hello?  Did this person skip 
elementary school in its entirety?  For anyone who might be wondering 
what is wrong with "your the moron", might I suggest a 
remedial course in contractions, and a careful study of the 
difference between "your" and "you're".

     This is pretty basic stuff here...  It drives me up the wall 
when people who should know better do these kind of things 
(especially journalists).  I remember when Jeffery Dahlmer was 
caught, our local paper ran the headline "Cannibal Killer 
Caught" and my first reaction was "which cannibals did he 
kill and why?"

     I generally don't point out these types of mistakes unless the 
person is in the process of flaming me, then I just do it to further 
enrage them, but it still bothers me.  What the hell are we teaching 
in school if someone who is competent to use a computer is unable to 
differentiate between "your" and "you're"?

    Its a sad comment...

PS - Yes, I'm sure you can find a typo or two in my posts.  I'm 
certainly not claiming to be the authority on English grammar, but 
then I'd never insist that "your the moron" was grammatical.
