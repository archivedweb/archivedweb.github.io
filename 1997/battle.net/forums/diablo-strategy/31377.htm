     DarkSniper,

     I have read all the threads on this post, and EVERYONE has valid 
points... But for the most part they're moot.

     Yes, him starting a guild when he doesn't know much about Diablo 
could be disastrous for some newbies.  And yes he should be allowed 
to play the game the way he wants.

     I understand your wanting to save some newbies pain (I was one 
once <grin>), but, if he really wants to go through with his 
clan, who are you to criticize him? You can point out that it might 
not be a good idea until he's more experienced, but browbeating it 
into him is NOT going to make it easier to take.

     As for all the debeting on cheating/cheaters and the flames 
flying back and forth, I don't know whether that's personal or not... 
So I wont comment on it.

     DarkSniper, think of it this way: When Diablo first came out, 
everyone was legit because nobody knew of or had any cheats.  Then 
people started finding them, which made the legits, unable to compete 
with the godly cheaters, form guilds and start playing private games. 
(BTW: I am not criticising guilds, I find the idea great... I'm just 
saying that cheaters have made people even more aware that they need 
people they can trust) Now, if we take this a step further and 
imagine your game:

     DarkSniper and (GuildLeader) are in a game. GuildLeader's guild 
is smaller and can't compete with DarkSniper's guild. Hence, 
GuildLeader either must conform to DarkSniper's standards or retreat 
to somewhere safe...

     Looked at another way:

     GuildLeader (Legits) is unable to compete with DarkSniper 
(cheaters) and must conform.

     AGAIN, I AM NOT CALLING YOU A CHEATER OR IMPLYING ANYTHING OF 
THE SORT.  I am just pointing out the similarities.  When confronted 
with a greater power, anyone can cave in. Don't tell me you might not 
have when you where starting your guild :).

     In conclusion, DS, all I'm trying to say is that you and 
everyone else is entitled to your own opinions, but your NOT entitled 
to ram them down someone else's throat :).

     Let the man play the way he wants to play.
