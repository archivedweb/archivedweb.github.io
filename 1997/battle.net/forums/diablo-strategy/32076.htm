If used properly, the guardian spell should not cause a problem
with the rogue or warrior.

The tatic I sometimes use (if I'm sure most of the monsters in the
room are not fire immune) is to flood the room with guardian. My
rogue and warrior buddies would stand by the door (not in front
of the door where firebolts may come out) and ambush anything
that comes out.

This works great for those rooms that are full of witches (except
soul burner). Use enough guardians and none of them will leave
the room alive.

This also works great in the Laz chamber. Only one (or may both)
of the unique witches are immune to fire and will crawl out of the
room. All the advocates and Laz can be killed if there are enough
guardians.
Of course, guardian should not be used in the same room the
characters are in. It's too much confusion.

My favorite staff is the Minecry. With 69 charges, I can go
guardian-happy and save my mana for mana shield. The best
part is it cost less than 1000 gp to recharge the staff.

Another thing, in a big room, the guardian usually does not
activate all the monsters in the area. It only shoots at monsters
that the guardian can see. And it doesn't miss often.

I hope this cast a new light on the guardian spell.
