I don't think it's silly at all.  As an old-time AD&D player, I too 
have been thinking for the last couple of months (since the PK 
debates) how the AD&D alignment system seems to fit the b.net 
environment (in a rough way). I also label the major axes as you do:  
Coop - PK  (corresponding to the Good-Evil axis) and Legit - Cheat 
(corresponding to the Law-Chaos axis).  Each axis is a continuum (as 
Lorax posted about the Legit-Cheat axis a few pages back), and each 
person (and perhaps clans) can be placed on the 2-dimensional plane 
according to their beliefs and actions.

Note that because each axis is a continuum, not all people who use 
"cheats" of one sort or another can be considered chaotic, 
etc.  For example, you classify Code Myster as a Chaotic Good.  Your 
Chaotic Good, however, includes all people who use cheats more than 
CM but do not PK (e.g., dupers, godmoders, hacked items, etc.).  I do 
not know exactly what cheats CM uses, but in recent posts he has 
admitted to cheating by (1) using backup programs, (2) teleporting in 
town, (3) using Deye, and (4) restarting games to go 
"shopping".  Number 4 should not be considered cheating (as I 
define it), Number 3 may or may not be cheating (opinion seems to be 
split), Number 2 is cheating, and Number 1 may or may not be 
cheating, depending on its use (again, opinion seems to be split).  
Since CM appears to use 1 and 3 to not gain an unfair advantage 
contrary to the spirit of the game (e.g., backing up after losing 
equipment to monsters), and since teleporting in town is relatively 
innocuous (I really can't see the 20 or so seconds saved as a 
significant advantage or disadvantage;  having a faster computer 
creates a much greater advantage in load times, etc.), I probably 
would place CM high on the "Co-op" end of that axis and 
towards the middle of the other.  In other words, Neutral Good. 

It's an interesting thought-experiment (and I'm glad to see a fellow 
Snake also has played AD&D . . . I'm willing to bet that most of us 
have).

See you around the Inferno Refugee Camp (aka the "Snakegod" 
channel) sometime!
