I'm at work.  I'm bored.  I'm crunching on carrot sticks and reading 
Ogden's when I should be drafting a document.  It's hot and sticky 
outside, but it's too cold in my office, and my stack of 
"professional reading" is warping my inbox.  So why am I here 
in Ogden's again?  

It's hard to believe that this game has been out for only about 6 
months (ok, when you add the pre-release Ogden's it's longer, and 
likewise the beta test, but still, roughly 6 months is all we have).  
To me, that's an unbelievably short time for all of us to get 
together and have the kind of fun that we've had.  My ICQ list has 
more than tripled lately, and I'm constantly amazed that with all of 
the folks I've met through Diablo, there's ALWAYS someone online to 
drop a casual "hello."  Maybe it's just me, but the fun in 
Ogden's and on B.Net has spread way beyond the game itself.  It's 
email, it's ICQ, it's the Snakegod channel, and yes, it's these lazy 
afternoons in Ogden's.

The game's great, yes, but I think the fact that this has given us 
all an excuse, or just the greater context, to get together and 
create all of this is absolutely wonderful.  Think about it - whether 
they make you smile or groan, so many people now are more than just 
character names to you.  Like it or not, you can pretty much guess 
the tone and content of a post or a whisper from Eugene, Tracy_Lords, 
Salmissra, CohentheBarbarian, S-R, Cronus, Kainazzo, Anti_Snoop, 
nIGHT, Lepthana, TheMan, P-R-O-D-I-G-Y, Kevin, Kalya, or Ridcully 
(not comprehensive, but a pretty scary mix, huh?).  For me, as a non-
computer-head at least, it's been a wonderfully weird ride so far.  

Let's hope it all keeps on clicking along.
