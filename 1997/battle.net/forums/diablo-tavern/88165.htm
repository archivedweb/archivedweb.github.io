	Aahh...nature. I understand completely what you mean by getting away 
from it all. I am fortunate enough to live practically smack dab in 
the middle of no where. I am surrounded by swamps, forest, grassy 
fields, and a beautiful river. My favourite past time in the summer 
here is to, at around dusk, climb up onto the top of my roof and 
watch the sun set (which is far more gorgeous than the ones you see 
on travel brochures), and then spend as much time as possible gazing 
up at the stars. If any one out there feels that humans are the most 
important species out there just spend fifteen minutes staring up at 
a clear night sky and you'll feel like the most insignificant form of 
life and you will LIKE it. This may sound crazy to many of you but if 
I was given the choice between having the latest technology, games, 
and enough money to make you a king or the ability and time to gaze 
up at the night sky, well, you just cannot beat those sparkling 
stars. Kiri-Jolith is one very smart person. We all need to stop 
every thing from time to time and just look at every thing we take 
for granted. How many of you can REALLY describe the texture of a 
stone or the feeling of soil falling between your fingers? How many 
of you can accurately describe the feeling of tree bark or the 
feeling of a tiny ant couragously crawling across your hand? How many 
of you stop for a significant amount of time to watch a spider spin a 
fresh web in the morning or stop to watch the feathers on a crow's 
wings flutter as it glides on a breeze?
