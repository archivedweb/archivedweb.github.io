I remember an old adage - unfortunately I can't remember where it 
comes from:

  "Take what you need and leave the rest as you found it."

This is the best (in my humble opinion) advice I have ever heard for 
using the resources around us, but one doesn't see it used very often 
in the world today.  I don't like to take political stances; I mostly 
meander through society taking the path of least resistance, but I 
have to say that I believe what I've read in these posts to be true.  
We, as a society, overuse the land around us, we take more than we 
need, and we waste most of what we take.  We as individuals don't go 
against society to stop this because, ... well,... who knows.  Maybe 
it's because it causes too much frustration.  Maybe it's because it 
takes too damn much energy.  Maybe we're content to just take the 
path of least resistance.  I can't speak for anyone but myself.

I care, and I think most of the people here care.  But, I'm not going 
to change the world tonight.  Instead, I think I'll take an 
inspiration from Stonehaven and go outside, look up at the stars, and 
see if I can still recognize some of the constellations.
