January... heh, the "good ole days"... Yeah they were
fun; and true, ironman does bring back alot of the excitement
of legit gaming. But time marches on my friend.  I think if you still 
know how to play the game, the present is just as much, if not
more fun then the past.  For instance...

While it is true, you no longer have the run of the place
(public games are pretty lame these days) if you know the
right people, you can still do all the old stuff.

Being legit is fun in and of itself.

Ironman rules.

If you miss the old flame warz, it is still easy enough to
rekindle them, here i'll start one for you:
theman, you suck, you cheat and you stink.  You are nothing
but a little boy on a power trip.
There that should about do it :)

The cheaters add a bit (a very little bit) of fun to the game,
they give you something to spam in chat, and something extra
to dodge while in a game.

Pk'rs are still the same old.  Nothing new, but again, avoiding
them in games has always been something i rather enjoyed.

Ghosts, the only crappy thing to have come of late.  They are
pretty much impossible to dodge, and they are generally real idiots
in chat.

Guilds, while not exactly new, they were pretty rare when the
game was first out (i can think of only 2 original guilds, Tarot and
inferno: good guys vs bad guys).  Guilds add a lot of fun to
the game.  There are inter guild wars, cross guild gaming co-ops,
and all sorts of other fun stuff.

Anyways, to cap it all off, i think the present is more fun then the
past.  There is more variety these days, and i think b.net is large
enough to accomidate it all.  (tho personnally i wish all the
cheaters, dupers, ghosts and softice addicts would drop
off the face of b.net forever... one can dream right?)
