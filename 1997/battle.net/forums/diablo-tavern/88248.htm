the result of a lot of small actions.  I can't change the 
amount of money that is spent on advertising, but I can be a 
responsible consumer and buy my products on the basis of the 
quality, not the glitz, or learn about the company and support
those that are environmentally responsible.  
  I can't rid the streets of crime, but I can get involved in 
youth groups and just perhaps make a tiny amount of headway 
against it.

making a difference, maybe we're not, but you have to keep trying.

  Esac mantioned that he was a parent, and that his time is taken
up in supporting and caring for his family, leaving the impilcation

I am also a parent, and I feel that providing a caring environment 
for my family IS making the world better.  Hopefully my kids have 
learned that other people are important, and they will in turn care 
for the people in their future.  

  The one thing you should not do is look at the problems of the 
world and despair.  If it a great concern of yours, then pick ONE 
problem and work on it.  Find organizations that support your view, 
and participate in trying to solve the problem.  Find politicians who 
agree with you and support them.  The fact that these organizations 
and politicians exist are a positive sign.  

  There are always going to be materialistic people out there who 
measure their happiness by how much they own.  You can't worry about 
them.  The vast majority of them will never accumulate enough to 
become satisfied, and will never learn to be happy with what they 
have.

  There are without a doubt people who use television and computer 
games as an escape from reality and responsibility.  But, you will 
not enrich their lives by rubbing their noses in the fact.  If this 
is the problem you wish to address, then help provide alternatives to 
these people.  Work with youth groups and help draw these kids to 
other activities. 
  There are also without a doubt, people who use television and 
reading and computer games as a diversion.  A way to relax, for fun 
and a sense of accomplishment.  There are positive aspects to these 
things.  Take Diablo for instance... [OOTC]  I enjoy the game, it 
fits well with my temperment and capabilities.  What am I proving by 
playing the game?  Well, that I can stick with things, that I'm 
willing to invest time in a goal I've set for myself.  If you are a 
coop'er you can help increase someone else's enjoyment, or just share 
it.  And I think these are worthwhile things.  
  My self worth is not tied to accomplishments in this game, but I 
can enjoy a sense of accomplishment in recovering my equipment from a 
sticky situation, or recognizing the futility of attempting, and 
going on to rebuild.
