Most Venerable Ridcully,

I, too, have become aware of a growing trend in the use of derivative
magic.

Many spells of this nature have been cast about this very
establishment! Many of the speakers here seem to have been affected
by an errant "Enlarged Dolt" spell, and could really use a 
spell of
"Enlightening". Of course, once these fellows begin to speak, 
one
feels the desire to cast "Don't Hearit", "Lock'o 
Lips", or
"Flame Knave". Some of this type are very proficient in the 
use of
"Gall'em". The more musical patrons entertain with spells of
"Accordion" and "Tuba".

This phenomenon is not limited to the patrons of Ogden's or even to
the bipedal Tristramians. Many of Tristram's youth, needing a quick
snack, have been asking derivative magicians to cast a complementary
"Crudbar". Cats and even cattle are impinged upon by this 
growing
trend, enduring spells of "Furball" and "Grazing". 
The religious
leaders attempt to sway us with "Holy Jolt", the comically 
endowed
with "Town Chortle", and the storytellers with "Ham 
Afield" (I know
it's a stretch). Some desparate adventuring d-mages have been observed
to summon immense mechanical hovering creatures. It is rumored that
the spell they use is called "Heliport". The recently player-
killed
have been noted frequently to cast "Prone Curse".

I will more fully document these and other spells in a formal report
to you at a later date.

Your humble part-time student,
ZelRiptha (Snakegod friend)
