In response to The Man's post about FFVII on page 2:

I've played through the Japanese version already (yes, I can speak 
and read Japanese very fluently), and although it is a great game, 
it's far from perfect.

Its one major flaw is the storyline, which starts off good, but 
becomes utterly horrible towards the end of the game. I won't get 
into specifics here, but lets just say that the end of the game was a 
major disappointment, considering how well done the first 2/3 was. 
This is something that I really hope is remedied in the American 
version. Considering all the work that's being put into it (Tetsuya 
Nomura is designing two new monsters for the American version of 
FFVII!), I wouldn't be surprised if they beefed up the story a bit. 
FFVII's linear nature will make it very difficult to for developers 
to make a drastic change though, and that's really what is needed. 

All of what I just said was confirmed in Gamefan some months ago by 
Casey Lo. I'm not making this up, people. This is a real problem that 
needs to be addressed.
