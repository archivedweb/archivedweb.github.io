Yep.  Roar was a complete disappointment.  Actors were shabby, plot 
was generic, and it overall exuded the worst qualities of Hercules or 
Xena (are there any GOOD qualities in either of those farces?).  

What could have been done better IMHO is primarily take more time on 
it.  Braveheart and other productions of the historical/fantasy genre 
have been most successful because they early on reproduce setting and 
atmosphere, then get the plot started (and I do NOT think Hercules or 
Xena either have any element of value).  If they were planning on 
following Braveheart, they should have taken another full hour to 
establish with the viewer some link with the characters and the 
setting.  Hell, I might as well have wasted an afternoon reading 
Dragonlance.

Another thing, the title was TERRIBLE.

HOWEVER, this show can still be salvaged (and not end up as another 
Space: Above and Beyond--another title which sucked).  First, make 
the characters more deep (and it's not too late to beg Patrick 
Stewart to play the Roman general leading the armies to come).  

If the plot lines are long and continuous, then this show could be a 
flaming success.  Most network television lacks this basic quality (X-
files almost did, soap-operas do too hehe...) which makes the show 
interesting and worth watching (unless they're yet another series 
planning on capitalizing on the typical American five-minute 
attention span).

I'll be watching the next two or three episodes. Let's all hope this 
gets better; there's almost nothing worth watching on TV for 
entertainment anymore.
