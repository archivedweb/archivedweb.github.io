(major clipping)

Yes, I remember you from long ago...   Back during the days of 
Bahamut's revolution, I was known as Kohre-Ahm.   I bet few INFERNOs 
today even remember me  (I was not particularly well known, nor was I 
out for impressions.   I PK'ed and enjoyed it, or at least thought I 
did)...

I just wanted to thank you for the advice you gave me, that at the 
time Kohre-Ahm was unwilling to listen to:  To leave INFERNO.  That 
advice sent me on a journey that ended with my admission into Ordo 
Rosae Moriatur and hence began the legacy that Hyleyn, who has never 
willingly taken the life of another character, has let bloom.

I now look back, and realize that my choice to take your advice was 
the best choice.   I don't think you remember Kohre-Ahm (he was one 
of the few INFERNO's who showed you any inkling of respect during 
Bahamut's revolution), but he remembers you.

Ordo Rosae Moriatur may not be the best known guild, but I found in 
it what I had been looking for on B.net;   Like-minded people 
gathered together to do more than just play Diablo.   To actually 
give Diablo a new life.

(in character now)

Let it be known that I plant a black rose in the garden of Ordo Rosae 
Moriatur to signify the passing of The Man, the person responsible 
for Kohre-Ahm's defection from INFERNO...   Everytime I look upon 
this rose, I shall remember what my father, Kohre-Ahm, had learned 
from you.   Rest well...

In Eternal Sincerity,

Hyleyn of Rythallha
The Black Rose of Ordo Rosae Moriatur
