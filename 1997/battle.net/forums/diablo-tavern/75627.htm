> Part 5
> 
> They were greeted by cool air and a smell of incense.  As they walked 
> inside Davion halted and looked about in wonder.  He had never seen a 
> place this beautiful.  This chamber was huge!  First thing he noticed 
> was the ceiling.  It loomed high above him and there was a painting 
> on it that depicted a huge battle.  On the painting he could see 
> hundreds of figures, angels and demons, fighting eachother.  He 
> couldnt understand how someone managed to paint it there!  It was so 
> high up and encompassed the entire ceiling!  Then Davion looked 
> around and noticed that the chamber was long and somewhat narrow.  
> Large widows with colored glass let plenty of light in and Davion 
> took a moment to admire the beautiful scene.  They resumed walking 
> and headed forward, towards an altar.  As they were approaching the 
> altar Davion gaped in wonder at the beautiful sculptures of angels 
> and fallen demons that adorned the walls.   
> 
> They soon reached the altar and Davions mother called to a boy who 
> was tending it.  He came to them briskly, and she asked him if she 
> could speak to a priest.  She said that its about her son Davion 
> joining the order...  The boy said that he is a priest, but in this 
> situation Davion and his mother need to talk to the high priestess.  
> While the boy and his mother were talking, Davion stared at the huge 
> statue of an angel that stood above the altar.  It was amazing.  
> Davion has never seen an angel before, but he could believe that it 
> was a real one, standing still.  It was beautiful.  It held a large 
> Broadsword just above its head, pointing it straight towards the 
> ceiling, with its wings stretched out, encompassing most of the 
> front wall.  He snapped out of his thoughts when his mother tugged 
> him to move.
> 
> They followed the boy into a medium size room, where he asked them to 
> sit down and wait.  He then left through another door.  As they sat 
> there, waiting, Davion and his mother curiously looked around the 
> room, intrigued by all sorts of religious items strewn about.  A few 
> minutes later the door opened again and the same boy came together 
> with an woman.  The woman introduced herself as Nahni, High 
> Priestess of the Temple of Tyrael while the boy went back into the 
> main chamber.  Nahni then walked up to Davion, stopped,  and peered 
> into his eyes.  Davions face turned red as he suddenly felt as if he 
> carried a lot of weight.  After what seemed to Davion a long, long 
> time, Nahni turned away and stood still.  She then asked him to wait 
> outside while she has a talk with his mother.
> 
> After Davion left the room, Nahni turned back and spoke to his 
> mother:  "He has a great fear and hatred of evil.  What happened 
> to him?"  Understanding dawned on Nahnis face as Davions 
> mother described the events that took place in Tristam barely a week 
> ago.   After her tale was done, Eleen (Davions mother) said that her 
> son wanted to join the Guards to become a warrior, but she wouldnt 
> let him.  Instead, she thought that he would be happy here, in this 
> great temple.  Nahni was silent.  She was lost in thoughts, 
> considering what she just saw and heard.  She knew that there was 
> more to this boy than met the eye.  For some reason, she recognized 
> him.  To Eleen, she then said:  We will take him into the order.  
> However, to cover our expenses and to support the church, we request 
> and require a fee of 50 gold pieces per month.  This fee will be 
> canceled if your son proves himself.
> 
> Eleen breathed a sigh of relief.  They accepted her son into the 
> order and even the fee wasnt that big.  She could now rest, knowing 
> that Davion was in good hands.  She was also proud of her son.  Being 
> accepted into this Temple was an honor and its members were looked 
> at with reverence.  She followed Nahni out of the room and into the 
> main chamber.  She saw Davion staring at Tyraels statue.  Eleen 
> smiled to herself.  She too loved to stare at it when she was small.
