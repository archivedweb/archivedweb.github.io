Three million a series eh?  Well i liked the show, but if i was 
producing it, not enough to spend 3 million a pop on it.
I guess if you are a fan of Hercules or Xena, and are used to seeing 
great special effects, out of this world monsters, and serious 
situations being made light of, then Roar is not for you.
Roar tries to add a bit (a real little bit) of realism to the hero 
genre.
Its setting in the Roman expansion period is a good one.  It does not 
over-use magic (something that drives me nuts because it romanticizes 
an otherwise ugly period in human history).  And the story apparently 
will have some overal grand plot (something both Herc and Xena lack).
Also i like the the use of actual biblical/historical figures.

Its true character developement is somewhat unoriginal (ok, very 
unoriginal), but i think the story still has a future.  I won't be 
dropping a final opinion on the show however till its aired at least 
2 or 3 more episodes.
