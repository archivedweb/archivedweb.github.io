Blizzard Entertainment Announces Starcraft at MacWorld


From the makers of the best-selling Warcraft series comes the next level in real-time strategy games as players journey into the reaches of space and the realms of Starcraft

BOSTON, Aug. 7, 1996 -- At MacWorld starting today, Blizzard Entertainment announced plans for a Macintosh version of Starcraft, a real-time strategy game of interstellar combat.

In Starcraft, players journey into the reaches of space to restore order to a galaxy on the brink interstellar warfare. Players command one of three distinct species -- Terrans, Protoss or Zerg -- as they complete 30 missions in space, on planetary surfaces and within command stations. With each mission, more of the epic story of the universe unfolds. The game includes multiplayer support for as many as eight players and one-click free access to on-line gaming via the company’s Battle.net service.

Starcraft will be available in Macintosh and Windows 95 CD-ROM formats at most computer and software retail chains nationwide for approximately $50. The game will also be available directly through Blizzard at (800) 953-SNOW.

Says Allen Adham, president and founder of Blizzard Entertainment, “We are drawn to the intense game play offered in the real-time strategy genre -- as demonstrated by our dedication and passion for the Warcraft series. With Starcraft, we have the opportunity to continue our tradition of offering highly addictive and exciting real-time games, while creating new worlds, experiences and technologies.”

Key features in Starcraft will include:
  • Three intergalactic species boasting unique units, technologies, attributes and abilities.
  • Evolving storyline with 30 missions that unfolds as players experience the game from each of the three species’ perspective.
  • Multiplayer option that supports as many as eight players via modem, network, direct link and the Internet.
  • Seamless one-click free access on-line gaming via Blizzard’s Battle.net service. Blizzard charges no hourly or monthly fees for game play. Player is responsible for any applicable Internet access fees.
  • Apple speech recognition technology
  • 3D sound with Apple Sound Sprocket (PowerMac with 16 MB RAM required)
