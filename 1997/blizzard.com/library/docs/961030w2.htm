Blizzard Entertainment's Warcraft II Remains Number-One Selling Title Of 1996

IRVINE, Calif., Oct. 30, 1996 -- According to PC Data's latest sales figures released this week, Blizzard Entertainment's Warcraft II: Tides of Darkness remains the number-one selling entertainment title of 1996.

With more than 1.2 million copies sold worldwide since shipping last December, Warcraft II is the breakaway game of the year. Year to date, Warcraft II is the top selling computer game. Additionally in monthly sales, Warcraft II remained the number-one selling PC and Macintosh entertainment title for September.

Warcraft II, the sequel to the company's award-winning fantasy-strategy game Warcraft: Orcs and Humans, is the continuation of the ruthless clash between the Orc and Human civilizations. As the commander of either the Orc or Human forces, players must successfully construct and rule their medieval empire while engaging in real-time tactical warfare over land, sea and air.

Warcraft II is available immediately in DOS and Macintosh CD-ROM format for approximately $50. Customers can call (800) 953-SNOW for additional information.

Best known for the number-one selling Warcraft series, Blizzard Entertainment is a premier publisher of entertainment software, and a division of Davidson & Associates, Inc., a wholly owned subsidiary of CUC International Inc. (NYSE: CU).

PC Data is the Reston, VA-based market research company that reports actual sell-through statistics for software in a wide range of categories.
