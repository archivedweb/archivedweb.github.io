Blizzard Entertainment is a premier publisher of entertainment software. Since establishing the Blizzard label in 1994, the company has quickly become one of the most popular and well respected makers of computer games.

The company's most recent release Diablo debuted at number one in the United States and was recently named Game of the Year. Diablo is the first title to take advantage of Blizzard's new on-line game service Battle.net, which allows customers to play games over the Internet for free. Since launching in January, Battle.net has become the world's largest on-line gaming service.

The company's number-one selling Warcraft series has won industry acclaim and has shattered sales records worldwide. Warcraft: Orcs and Humans received accolades as one of the best strategy games of 1994. The game's blockbuster sequel Warcraft II: Tides of Darkness has sold nearly two million copies worldwide and has won Game of the Year as well as Best Multiplayer Game.

Blizzard's upcoming titles include Starcraft and Warcraft Adventures: Lord of the Clans. Starcraft, which is scheduled to release later this year, is a real-time space strategy game in which players restore order to a galaxy on the brink of interstellar warfare. Warcraft Adventures: Lord of the Clans, due out Christmas 1997, is a fantasy adventure game that reveals the pivotal next chapter in the epic Warcraft saga.

Prior to the release of Warcraft, the company served as a third-party developer creating entertainment software for a variety of platforms including DOS, Macintosh, Sega Genesis and Super Nintendo. Some of the company's best-known titles include Rock 'n Roll Racing, The Lost Vikings, Blackthorne and The Death and Return of Superman.

The Blizzard research and development group is comprised of 75 designers, producers, programmers, artists and sound engineers. Each member of the Blizzard staff is a true game enthusiast, which provides the company invaluable insight to creating products for the gaming industry.

In March 1996, Blizzard acquired Blizzard North, a concentrated team of 25 developers specializing in high-quality entertainment products for computer and home console systems. Located in Redwood City, Calif., Blizzard North strives to push gaming technology to the next level.

Headquartered in Irvine, Calif., Blizzard Entertainment was founded in 1990 under the name Silicon & Synapse by President Allen Adham and Vice President Michael Morhaime. Blizzard is now a division of CUC Software, an operating unit of CUC International Inc. (NYSE:CU)
